Problem Analysis
U ser research with 25 Italian homeowners revealed the core challenges. 42% of smart lock reviews cite battery failures as the primary concern; 32% identify trust deficit as the main adoption barrier.

The anxiety-trust paradox emerged: daily door-checking anxiety conflicts with fear of technology failure. Italian homeowners have invested significantly in porta blindata systems and distrust solutions that replace rather than integrate. ’

Five user archetypes identified. Personas: Claudio the Record-Keeper and Marco represent the security-anxiety demographic. Market data from Puglia (Bari's burglary rates) informs willingness-to-purchase analysis.

Key insight: Integration with existing security infrastructure resonates better than replacement. EU privacy compliance and local biometric storage are essential. “”

Ideation
Volturio's key differentiators: hardwired power (eliminating battery anxiety), local biometric storage (EU privacy compliance), seamless integration with existing Italian security infrastructure.

Premium market positioning: €3,500–€8,000 price range reflects quality expectations of Italian homeowners. Lean Canvas models and competitive analysis developed. Team roles: Demyan (prototype development, project management, market research), Federico Resta (presentation, marketing).

Human-centered design methodology: Understand, Observe, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test. Privacy-first design principles. Professional aesthetics matching Italian brands like BTicino for market acceptance.

Success measured through comprehensive methodology—from initial user research through prototype testing. Academic deliverables serve as project milestones.

DEVELOPMENT & ITERATIONS
Development & Prototype:

Cost analysis: €579.50 for 7-day academic prototype, €265,000 for 6-month business MVP. Prototype testing with both in-class and external users validated the core concept.
Recent testing revealed user concerns center on implementation costs rather than functionality—validating the concept while highlighting need for clear value communication.
Design evolved toward low-profile aesthetic: smart lock visually indistinguishable from traditional door hardware while preserving advanced features.
Progression to Final Prototype:


Iterations refined the design based on user feedback. The final prototype balances advanced biometric technology with familiar, trustworthy aesthetics. Local storage addresses privacy; hardwired power addresses reliability concerns.

Tools: Google Forms, Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Tome, Canva for presentations. Market research from NeighborhoodScout, Amazon reviews, Italian security forums, European Parliament regulatory documents.

Complete business documentation: Lean Canvas, competitive analysis, comprehensive cost breakdowns. The learning journey from research to prototype—documented.
